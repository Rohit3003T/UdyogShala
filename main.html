Market Research and Niche Identification

  • Promotes entrepreneurship, providing loans from ₹10 lakhs to ₹1 crore for first-time businesses.

  • Credit-linked subsidy scheme promoting self-employment ventures in the micro-enterprise sector.

Machinery Operation and Training

  • Machinery for Candle Making
  • Candle Making Process

Machinery for Candle Making:

  • Melting Pot or Double Boiler: A fundamental tool for efficiently melting wax, ensuring a consistent and smooth texture.
  • Thermometer: An indispensable device that monitors the wax temperature, contributing to the precision of the candle-making process.
  • Pouring Pitcher: This specialized tool facilitates the controlled pouring of melted wax into molds, allowing for intricate and detailed candle designs.
  • Wick Holder or Wick Bar: Ensures the proper centering of the wick during the pouring stage, a crucial aspect for an even burn.
  • Molds: Available in various shapes and sizes, molds play a pivotal role in determining the final form of the candles, allowing for creativity and customization.
  • Cooling Rack: After the pouring process, a cooling rack is employed to enable the candles to cool and harden uniformly, contributing to their structural integrity.
  • Wick Trimmer: A precision tool designed for post-production, trimming wicks to the desired length for an optimal burn.
  • Scale: Accurate measurement is paramount in candle making. A reliable scale is essential for measuring wax and fragrance oil, ensuring consistency in each batch.
  • Stirring Utensils: These tools facilitate thorough mixing of wax and additives, ensuring a homogenous mixture and enhancing the quality of the candles.
  • Heat Source: Whether it's a stove or a dedicated hot plate, the heat source is the powerhouse driving the wax-melting process.

Candle Making Process:

  1. Prepare the Work Area: Begin with a clean and organized workspace, ensuring a conducive environment for the meticulous craft of candle making.
  2. Measure Ingredients: Accurate measurement is the foundation of successful candle making.
  3. Melting Wax: Utilize a melting pot or double boiler to melt the wax, maintaining a keen eye on the temperature to achieve the desired consistency.
  4. Adding Fragrance: Enhance your candles with aromatic notes by carefully incorporating fragrance oil into the melted wax, ensuring a harmonious blend.
  5. Preparing Molds: Arrange the molds, strategically placing wicks and securing them with a wick holder to maintain proper alignment during the pouring process.
  6. Pouring Wax: Exercise precision as you pour the melted wax into the molds, creating the foundation for the unique shapes and designs of your candles.
  7. Cooling: Transfer the molds to a cooling rack, allowing the candles to cool and solidify evenly, a crucial step for the final product's structural integrity.
  8. Trimming Wicks: Employ a wick trimmer to cut the wicks to the desired length, ensuring an optimal burn and a polished aesthetic.
  9. Removing Candles: Gently release the candles from the molds, unveiling the fruits of your labor.
  10. Quality Check: Thoroughly inspect each candle, checking for any imperfections or defects. This step ensures that your creations meet the highest standards.
