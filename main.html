Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY):

  • Offers loans up to ₹10 lakh for small businesses, categorized into Shishu (up to ₹50,000), Kishor (up to ₹5 lakhs), and Tarun (up to ₹10 lakhs).

Stand-Up India Scheme:

  • Promotes entrepreneurship, providing loans from ₹10 lakhs to ₹1 crore for first-time businesses.
  • Encourages loans to female borrowers, and funds can be used for starting or expanding businesses.

Credit Guarantee Fund Scheme for Micro and Small Enterprises (CGS):

  • Offers collateral-free loans up to ₹2 crores for MSEs without third-party guarantees.
  • National Credit Guarantee Trustee Company (NCGTC) provides credit guarantee cover, reducing risks for banks.

Pradhan Mantri Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP):

  • Credit-linked subsidy scheme promoting self-employment ventures in the micro-enterprise sector.
  • Facilitates new businesses, providing financial assistance through banks with subsidies ranging from 15% to 35%.

Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI) Schemes:

  • SIDBI offers various schemes, including CGTMSE for collateral-free loans and SMILE for manufacturing businesses.
  • Stand-Up India under SIDBI supports SC/ST and women entrepreneurs with loans between ₹10 lakhs and ₹1 crore.

Export Credit Guarantee Corporation (ECGC) Schemes:

  • Provides small business loans for exporters, offering credit insurance against non-payment delays.
  • Offers pre-shipment and post-shipment finance, facilitating international business expansion.

State-Specific Loan Schemes:

Machinery for Candle Making:

  1. Melting Pot or Double Boiler:
    A fundamental tool for efficiently melting wax, ensuring a consistent and smooth texture.
  2. Thermometer:
    An indispensable device that monitors the wax temperature, contributing to the precision of the candle-making process.
  3. Pouring Pitcher:
    This specialized tool facilitates the controlled pouring of melted wax into molds, allowing for intricate and detailed candle designs.
  4. Wick Holder or Wick Bar:
    Ensures the proper centering of the wick during the pouring stage, a crucial aspect for an even burn.
  5. Molds:
    Available in various shapes and sizes, molds play a pivotal role in determining the final form of the candles, allowing for creativity and customization.
  6. Cooling Rack:
    After the pouring process, a cooling rack is employed to enable the candles to cool and harden uniformly, contributing to their structural integrity.
  7. Wick Trimmer:
    A precision tool designed for post-production, trimming wicks to the desired length for an optimal burn.
  8. Scale:
    Accurate measurement is paramount in candle making. A reliable scale is essential for measuring wax and fragrance oil, ensuring consistency in each batch.
  9. Stirring Utensils:
    These tools facilitate thorough mixing of wax and additives, ensuring a homogenous mixture and enhancing the quality of the candles.
  10. Heat Source:
    Whether it's a stove or a dedicated hot plate, the heat source is the powerhouse driving the wax-melting process.

Candle Making Process:

  1. Prepare the Work Area:
    Begin with a clean and organized workspace, ensuring a conducive environment for the meticulous craft of candle making.
  2. Measure Ingredients:
    Accurate measurement is the foundation of successful candle making. Weigh and measure wax and fragrance oil precisely.
  3. Melting Wax:
    Utilize a melting pot or double boiler to melt the wax, maintaining a keen eye on the temperature to achieve the desired consistency.
  4. Adding Fragrance:
    Enhance your candles with aromatic notes by carefully incorporating fragrance oil into the melted wax, ensuring a harmonious blend.
  5. Preparing Molds:
    Arrange the molds, strategically placing wicks and securing them with a wick holder to maintain proper alignment during the pouring process.
  6. Pouring Wax:
    Exercise precision as you pour the melted wax into the molds, creating the foundation for the unique shapes and designs of your candles.
  7. Cooling:
    Transfer the molds to a cooling rack, allowing the candles to cool and solidify evenly, a crucial step for the final product's structural integrity.
  8. Trimming Wicks:
    Employ a wick trimmer to cut the wicks to the desired length, ensuring an optimal burn and a polished aesthetic.
  9. Removing Candles:
    Gently release the candles from the molds, unveiling the fruits of your labor.
  10. Quality Check:
    Thoroughly inspect each candle, checking for any imperfections or defects. This step ensures that your creations meet the highest standards.
